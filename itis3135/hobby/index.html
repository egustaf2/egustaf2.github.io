What is Powerlifting?

Powerlifting is a strenght sport that consists of three attemps at maximal weight on three lifts; squat, bench, and deadlift. In powerlifting, you can compete equipped or raw. Competing raw means you are only allowed to use an approved weight belt, an approved singlet, approved wrist wraps and chalk. Competing equipped allows you to use knee wraps, a squat suit, and a bench shirt in additon to the items allowed by raw competitors.

Some Powerlifting Federations:

  • International PowerLifting Federation: IPF
  • United States Powerlifting Association: USPA
  • USA Powerlifting: USAPL

Learn how to Squat

Different ways to squat:

  • High bar
  • Low bar

Some things to keep in mind while Squatting:

  1. Bar at chest height
  2. Create tention in upper body and lower body
  3. Hands in a good position
  4. Pull down on the barbell
  5. Create tension and take deep a deep breath
  6. Take a few steps back
  7. Keep feet slightly turned out to the side
  8. External rotation torque, knees out to the side
  9. Feet rooted into the groud
  10. Breath into your gut

Learn how to Bench

Different ways sto bench:

  • Big arch
  • Small arch

Some things to keep in mind while Benching:

  1. Retract your shoulders
  2. Points of contact on bench
  3. Natural lower back arch or slightly exagerated back arch
  4. Bench press grip (hand placement)
  5. Barbell path
  6. Generating power with foot placement and leg drive
  7. Breathing and bracing

Learn how to Deadlift

Different ways to Deadlift:

  • Conventional
  • Sumo

Some things to keep in mind while Deadlifting:

  1. Deadlifting grip; mixed grip, double overhand, hook grip
  2. Grip placement
  3. Stance
  4. Hip position
  5. Leg drive
  6. Head placement
  7. Neautral spine
